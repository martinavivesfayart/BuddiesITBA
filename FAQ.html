Before Your Arrival

You can sign in for a spanish course given at ITBA, it starts two weeks before the beggining of the semester. But you should know it has an additional cost. You can continue with your course during the semester

You can choose courses from all the programs. Once the semester starts, you will have two weeks to decide whether you like the course or not, and make changes to your course program.

Once you're Here

ITBA has three buildings: Sede Financiero, Sede Rectorado and Sede Distrito Tecnológico. The first one is located in the neighborhood of Microcentro, while the other ones are in Parque Patricios. You can have classes in any of them, depending on the courses you choose

Besides, ITBA has many student groups with different themes. They often have events and activities you can join.

- There are microwaves in the university, in case you need to bring your lunch. But if you don’t bring your own, you can also buy food inside the buldings, or at any restaurant close to them and bring it back to the university.

- You can join the CEITBA organization, which has different discounts on gyms, and offers some sport classes, such as football, swimming lessons and basketball.

Where to Live

Buenos Aires has many neighborhoods where is possible to stay, each with is’s own vibe. Here are some of the most popular neighborhoods of the city:

  • Palermo: Known for its trendy vibe, Palermo is divided into several sub-neighborhoods, including Palermo Soho and Palermo Hollywood. It’s famous for its great nightlife, big parks like the Bosques de Palermo and its numerous cafes, restaurants, and art galleries.
  • Recoleta: This is one of Buenos Aires' most upscale neighborhoods, known for its elegant architecture and historic charm. Highlights include the Recoleta Cemetery and the nearby Centro Cultural Recoleta. The area is also home to high-end shopping and dining options.
  • Belgrano: A residential area with leafy streets and elegant homes. Known for its good schools, and a variety of dining and shopping options along Avenida Cabildo. It has a family-friendly, suburban feel.

    Public Transport

  • Metro: There is a big subway system in Buenos Aires, called “Subte”. It reaches most of the city and is quite easy to use. It works efficiently and quickly, having a minimum frequency of approx. 3 minutes, depending on the subte line.
  • Bus: Buenos Aires has a huge amount of bus lines. It can take you pretty much wherever you want to go, but it is less reliable than the subte, since it is not as regular. Each stop has a pole with the number of the line. In order to stop the bus, you have to raise your arm.
  • Train: There are four train companies, each for a different zone of the province of Buenos Aires. The train is mainly helpful for going outside of the capital, reaching places like Tigre. Each company has many branches with different stations, so make sure you get on right one!
    • SUBE CARD
    In order to take any public transport, you will need this card. It can be bought and charged in some metro stations as well as kiosks, mostly with cash.
    Once you’ve got your SUBE, register it in the sube app, if not, you’ll be charged double!.

    Private Transport

    If you don’t want to use the public transport, you can use apps like Uber, Cabify or Didi. Besides, there are many taxis in the city, but make sure they don’t do a longer route to charge you more. You can also order a taxi with their app, BA Taxi.

    Travelling Around

    Buenos Aires has two main airports: Ministro Pistarini International Airport (commonly known as Ezeiza) and Jorge Newbery Airport (Aeroparque)

  • Ezeiza airport (EZE): located in the city of Ezeiza, about 35km south of Buenos Aires, it is the bigest airport of Argentina. It is possible to get there in public transport either by bus line 8, or with the “Airport Express” bus service, which offers a direct route from the city center. Private shuttles and airport transfers are also available for pre-booking.
  • Aeroparque Airport (AEP): this airport handles domestic and some regional flights and is located just a few kilometers from the city center. You can easily reach AEP by taxi or ride- sharing services, which are readily available. Public transport options include taking Line A of the Buenos Aires Metro to the "Córdoba" station and then transferring to Bus 33, 37, 45, or 160. Additionally, airport shuttles can be booked in advance for a direct connection.
